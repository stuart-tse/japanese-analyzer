Design Version: v2024-08-26 | WCAG Compliance: AA Level

Design Rationale

Cultural Inspiration: Colors draw from traditional Japanese aesthetics including sumi-e ink painting, indigo dyeing (ai-zome), and natural materials like bamboo, cedar, and handmade paper.

Accessibility Focus: All color combinations meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards with contrast ratios ≥4.5:1 for normal text and ≥3:1 for large text. Special attention paid to color-blind accessibility.

Eye Strain Reduction: Warm undertones in backgrounds, reduced blue light emission, and carefully calibrated brightness levels for extended reading sessions.

Grammar Distinction: Each part of speech uses distinct hues with sufficient color space between them, while maintaining harmony within the overall palette.

Usage Guidelines

Background Hierarchy

  • Primary Background (#0D1117): Main app background, behind all content
  • Surface Background (#161B22): Cards, modals, elevated panels
  • Secondary Surface (#1C2128): Input fields, secondary cards, nested content

Text Hierarchy

  • Primary Text (#F0F4F8): Body text, main content
  • Secondary Text (#E2E8F0): Headings, emphasized content
  • Muted Text (#A0AEC0): Captions, metadata, secondary information
  • Disabled Text (#718096): Inactive elements, placeholders

Accessibility Notes

  • All combinations exceed WCAG 2.1 AA standards (4.5:1 for normal text)
  • Grammar colors are distinguishable for common types of colorblindness
  • Interactive elements have sufficient touch target contrast
  • Focus indicators use high contrast combinations

Best Practices

  • Use grammar colors consistently across all analysis features
  • Maintain the 3-level background hierarchy for visual depth
  • Apply accent colors sparingly for maximum impact
  • Test color combinations in actual usage scenarios
  • Consider user preferences for color intensity adjustments
